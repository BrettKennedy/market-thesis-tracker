# Investment Policy

## Objective
Use thematic research and disciplined monitoring to allocate a defined portion of capital into high-conviction market ideas.

## Portfolio Philosophy
- Long-term wealth building first
- Thematic exposure over isolated stock picking
- Willing to own volatility if fundamentals support it
- Prefer staged deployment over all-in entries
- Use trimming and position sizing to manage risk, not prediction confidence alone

## Sleeve Structure
This project governs a dedicated thematic investing sleeve, not the entire portfolio.

## Capital Deployment Rules
- New themes start with a starter allocation, not full size
- Scale only after the framework survives at least one full review cycle or earnings cycle
- Maintain some dry powder unless conviction and confirmation are unusually strong

## Position Size Guidelines
### Core names
- Larger allowed position size
- Used for durable, higher-quality exposure to the theme

### Torque names
- Smaller allowed position size
- Used for asymmetric upside and operating leverage
- Assume high drawdown risk

### Canaries
- Usually tracked, not necessarily owned
- Used to test whether the thesis is strengthening or weakening

## Sizing Framework
Default guidelines:
- Max size for a core position: 5% of thematic sleeve
- Max size for a torque position: 3% of thematic sleeve
- Max initial size for a new torque position: 1% to 2% of thematic sleeve
- Cash or cash-like reserve in sleeve: 20% to 40% until framework is proven

## Add Rules
Add only when at least one of the following is true:
- thesis health is improving
- fundamental evidence improved materially
- earnings confirmed the thesis and guidance strengthened
- market weakness created a better entry without breaking the thesis

Do not add solely because a stock is up.

## Trim Rules
Trim when one or more of the following is true:
- price has run far ahead of fundamentals
- position size has grown beyond intended risk
- earnings were “good enough” but no longer improving
- crowding and valuation appear extreme
- another expression of the same theme is clearly better

## Stop Adding Rules
Pause new capital when:
- multiple signals weaken across the same theme
- management tone softens across several basket names
- market reaction turns consistently negative to solid results
- disconfirming evidence is accumulating faster than confirming evidence

## Sell / Cut Rules
Reduce or exit when:
- the thesis is clearly broken
- the company-specific story no longer expresses the original thesis
- execution problems persist across multiple quarters
- the risk/reward is clearly inferior to another expression of the same theme

## Evidence Hierarchy
Priority goes to:
1. filings
2. earnings releases and calls
3. management guidance
4. customer concentration and backlog disclosures
5. market reaction in context

## Prediction Discipline
Every meaningful position or thesis decision should be logged with:
- date
- thesis
- expected outcome
- what would prove it wrong
- later review of what actually happened
