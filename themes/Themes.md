# Themes

## Theme 1: AI Infrastructure Buildout Is Durable

### Thesis Statement
Over the next 4 to 6 quarters, AI-related data-center spending remains net expansionary because the binding constraints are physical: electrical capacity, thermal management, network fabric, and optical interconnect. The thesis does not require a single model winner. It requires that infrastructure bottlenecks remain real across multiple layers of the stack.

### Why This Matters
This theme is useful only if it captures demand that survives model leadership changes. The goal is to own the most durable beneficiaries of AI buildout rather than chase whichever model company currently has momentum.

### Confirmation Signals
- Power and cooling vendors report sustained demand, order strength, or backlog support
- Networking vendors continue to cite real AI cluster growth rather than generic AI interest
- Optics and interconnect vendors show ramp, mix improvement, or margin support tied to AI deployments
- Data-center capacity remains constrained or strategically valuable in key markets
- Strength persists across at least three of these four layers: power, networking, optics, capacity owners

### Disconfirming Signals
- Two or more direct-exposure names guide down on AI demand in the same review cycle
- AI demand language weakens across several sub-sectors at once
- Power or cooling lead times normalize quickly and backlog rolls over
- Optics or interconnect ramps stall or show broad digestion
- REIT leasing weakens while direct vendors remain promotional

### Strongest Counter-Narrative
The buildout is front-loaded rather than durable. Hyperscalers and enterprises over-ordered, efficiency improves faster than expected, and the market is mistaking one-time cluster land grabs for a multi-year compounding cycle.

- VRT
- ANET
- ETN

## Theme 2: SaaS Shakeout Is Real but Selective

### Thesis Statement
Over the next 4 to 6 quarters, AI does not break software broadly. It shifts value toward workflow owners, system-of-record platforms, and multi-product suites with embedded distribution. The likely losers are narrower point solutions with weaker pricing power, lower switching costs, and less control of the core workflow.

### Why This Matters
This theme is meant to separate durable software franchises from software names that look fine until retention, bookings, or monetization quality deteriorate. It is not a blanket short thesis on software.

### Confirmation Signals
- Platform and workflow owners maintain growth, cash flow, and customer expansion
- Challenged point solutions show weaker retention, billings, or longer deal cycles
- AI features become table stakes rather than durable pricing levers
- Customers rationalize software spend and consolidate vendors
- Clear separation appears between winners and challenged names rather than broad software strength

### Disconfirming Signals
- Supposedly challenged names reaccelerate together
- Net retention or billings improve across the weaker cohort at the same time
- AI clearly expands software budgets across the board rather than intensifying competition
- Sales efficiency improves broadly in previously challenged names
- Platform winners fail to separate on growth, obligations, or cash generation

### Strongest Counter-Narrative
AI expands software value creation instead of compressing it. Incumbents and even many point solutions absorb AI quickly enough that the feared shakeout does not arrive, or arrives much later than expected.

### Benchmark
- NOW
- CRM
- MSFT

### Control / Canaries
- ADBE
- MDB
- TWLO

## Theme Review Rules
Each monthly theme review should include:
- what changed
- what still holds
- what is weakening
- strongest counter-narrative
- current best expressions of the theme
- current canaries
- revised theme health score
- what would change the score next month
