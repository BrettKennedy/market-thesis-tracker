# Themes

## Theme 1: AI Infrastructure Buildout Is Durable

### Thesis Statement
AI infrastructure buildout remains robust over the next several years because compute demand, data center power requirements, networking intensity, and optical/interconnect complexity continue to rise even if model leadership rotates among companies.

### Why This Matters
This theme is not dependent on a single model company winning. It is based on the idea that power, cooling, networking, optics, and rack-scale connectivity remain bottlenecks regardless of which foundation model provider is currently strongest.

### Confirmation Signals
- Hyperscaler capex remains elevated or rises
- Power and cooling vendors report sustained demand and healthy backlogs
- Networking vendors continue to cite AI cluster growth
- Optics/interconnect vendors show real ramp and improving margins
- Data center capacity remains tight or strategically valuable

### Disconfirming Signals
- Multiple hyperscalers guide capex down at the same time
- AI-related demand language weakens across several sub-sectors
- Power/cooling lead times normalize quickly
- Optics/interconnect ramps stall or show widespread digestion
- Market stops rewarding strong AI infra earnings and guides

### Benchmark
- QQQ
- SOXX

### Control / Canaries
- Traditional industrials without strong AI linkage
- Rate-sensitive data center REITs
- Lower-quality optics names or overextended infra names

## Theme 2: SaaS Shakeout Is Real but Selective

### Thesis Statement
AI is increasing pressure on weaker SaaS names by compressing product differentiation, increasing customer scrutiny, and shifting value toward platforms and workflow owners. The damage is real, but not universal. Strong platforms can consolidate share.

### Why This Matters
This theme helps distinguish between durable software businesses and vulnerable app-layer names that may look fine until growth, retention, or sales efficiency start slipping.

### Confirmation Signals
- Weaker SaaS names show declining net retention, bookings weakness, or longer deal cycles
- Stronger platform names maintain growth, cash flow, and customer expansion
- AI features become table stakes rather than durable pricing levers
- Customers rationalize software spend and consolidate vendors

### Disconfirming Signals
- Weaker SaaS names broadly reaccelerate together
- Net retention improves across the weaker cohort
- AI clearly expands software budgets across the board rather than intensifying competition
- Sales efficiency improves broadly in challenged names

### Benchmark
- QQQ

### Control / Canaries
- Strong platform software names
- High-multiple but operationally weaker SaaS names
- Select developer or data-layer names that could either benefit or get squeezed
